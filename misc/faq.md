# Frequently asked questions

## Is PULSAR a stablecoin?

No. PULSAR is intended to be a free-floating, treasury-backed protocol token. It does not target a one-dollar peg.

## Is PULSAR guaranteed to trade above its backing?

No. Market price can trade above or below any accounting measure of backing. Treasury assets may also be volatile, illiquid, bridged, or exposed to smart-contract risk.

## What is QUASAR?

QUASAR is the staked representation of PULSAR. Its final reward and conversion behavior will be defined by the deployed contracts.

## Will there be wrapped QUASAR?

A non-rebasing wrapper is planned for easier DeFi integration, but its final name, ticker, network support, and contract are not yet available.

## Where do staking rewards come from?

They may come from authorized PULSAR emissions, treasury-funded mechanisms, or both. The dashboard and policy documentation should label the source. Token emissions are not the same as treasury earnings.

## What assets can I use to mint?

Possible assets include pDAI, WPLS, USDC, PLSX, ETH or WETH, approved LP positions, and assets added later. Only markets shown in the verified application are active.

## Is minting always cheaper than buying?

No. A displayed discount can disappear if PULSAR's price changes during vesting. Compare the effective mint price, time risk, fees, liquidity, and contract risk with a spot purchase.

## What is (3,3)?

It is shorthand for aligned participation. Pulsar reframes it as staking when appropriate, participating in value-accretive mints, building the ecosystem, and compounding productive treasury capital. It is not an instruction never to sell.

## Does the treasury belong directly to token holders?

The treasury is controlled under the protocol's contracts and governance. Holding PULSAR does not automatically create a legal or contractual right to redeem a share of treasury assets.

## Are treasury distributions guaranteed?

No. Distributions depend on actual realized earnings, losses, expenses, reserve requirements, authorization, legal considerations, and the final contract system.

## Are the contract addresses in these docs usable?

No. Every current address is an obvious placeholder. Wait for verified deployments and independently confirm addresses through official channels.

## Has Pulsar been audited?

Audit status is pending. An audit reduces some risks but cannot prove that contracts, governance, markets, or treasury strategies are safe.
