# Protocol-owned liquidity

Protocol-owned liquidity, or **POL**, is liquidity that the Pulsar treasury owns rather than temporarily rents from outside providers.

## Why own liquidity?

Owning liquidity can help the protocol:

- maintain deeper markets for PULSAR;
- earn a share of swap fees;
- reduce dependence on short-lived liquidity incentives;
- manage liquidity positions as long-term treasury assets;
- support integrations that require reliable on-chain markets.

## How Pulsar may acquire it

The treasury may receive LP positions through liquidity mints, create positions with treasury assets, or acquire them through another authorized process. Each pool should be disclosed by network, exchange, pair, fee tier, position type, and contract address.

## Risks and valuation

POL is not risk-free backing. It can face impermanent loss, smart-contract exploits, concentration, low trading volume, incentive changes, price manipulation, and exit slippage.

When one side of a position is PULSAR, counting the entire LP market value as independent backing can exaggerate treasury strength. Dashboard methodology should identify circular exposure and publish conservative valuation assumptions.

The objective is useful, durable liquidity—not the largest possible headline POL number.
