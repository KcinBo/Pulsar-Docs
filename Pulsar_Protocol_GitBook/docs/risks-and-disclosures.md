# Risks and disclosures

Pulsar Protocol is experimental crypto infrastructure. Participation can result in a partial or total loss. Nothing in these docs is financial, investment, legal, accounting, or tax advice.

## Smart-contract risk

Code may contain errors or behave differently from user expectations. Audits can reduce certain risks but cannot guarantee safety. Proxies, upgrade keys, multisigs, emergency roles, oracles, and integrations add additional failure modes.

## Token and dilution risk

PULSAR may be highly volatile. Staking rewards may be funded partly or entirely by new token issuance. A growing QUASAR balance does not guarantee growing market value or purchasing power.

## Treasury and strategy risk

Treasury assets can fall in value. Productive strategies can suffer exploits, depegs, impermanent loss, liquidation, bad debt, counterparty failure, or operational mistakes. Realized losses and expenses can eliminate distributable earnings.

## Liquidity risk

Markets may be thin or disappear. Large transactions may experience severe slippage. Protocol-owned liquidity may be concentrated, difficult to unwind, or exposed to PULSAR on both sides of its valuation.

## Stablecoin and bridged-asset risk

A token called DAI, USDC, ETH, or another familiar symbol may be a bridged or third-party representation with different issuers and risks. Stablecoins can depeg. Bridges can be exploited, paused, censored, or become illiquid.

## Minting and vesting risk

A mint discount can become a loss while PULSAR vests. Pricing can depend on oracles, pool liquidity, policy parameters, and contract logic. A participant may be unable to exit immediately.

## Governance and key-holder risk

Authorized actors may change parameters, move treasury assets, upgrade contracts, pause systems, or make poor decisions. Governance can be captured by concentrated voting power or compromised credentials.

## Regulatory, legal, and tax risk

Rules vary by location and can change. Token issuance, staking, treasury management, and distributions may create legal, reporting, or tax obligations. Obtain advice appropriate to your jurisdiction.

## Information risk

Documentation, interfaces, dashboards, and third-party data can be incorrect or delayed. Contract behavior controls when documentation conflicts with verified deployed code.

## No guarantees

Pulsar does not guarantee a price floor, redemption value, APY, rebase rate, treasury return, distribution, liquidity level, audit outcome, or continuation of any policy. Use only capital you can afford to lose and independently verify every contract and transaction.
