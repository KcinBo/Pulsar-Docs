# Treasury earnings and distributions

Pulsar is designed around a simple idea: grow protocol-owned capital, then make transparent decisions about what to do with the earnings that capital produces.

## What counts as earnings?

Clear definitions prevent token emissions or deposits from being mislabeled as revenue.

- **Realized income:** fees, interest, incentives, or other income actually received.
- **Realized gains or losses:** profit or loss recognized when a position is closed or settled.
- **Unrealized gains or losses:** changes in estimated market value while a position remains open.
- **Net treasury earnings:** realized income and gains, minus realized losses, operating costs, and approved risk provisions.
- **Distributable earnings:** the portion of net earnings available after reserve requirements and other policy obligations.

New assets received through mints are treasury inflows, not investment earnings. Newly issued PULSAR is an emission, not outside revenue.

## Possible uses of earnings

Subject to final governance, contracts, and legal review, realized earnings may be used to:

- compound the treasury;
- replenish liquid reserves;
- cover protocol operations;
- deepen protocol-owned liquidity;
- buy PULSAR from the market;
- burn or redistribute purchased PULSAR;
- fund staking or claimable distributions;
- combine several of these actions.

## No automatic promise

The treasury may experience periods with no positive realized earnings. Losses, expenses, reserve needs, liquidity requirements, and market conditions may reduce or eliminate amounts available for distribution. A distribution should not be announced until it is funded and authorized.

If a distribution system launches, the documentation should publish:

1. the measurement period;
2. the assets and strategies included;
3. how gains, losses, expenses, and provisions are calculated;
4. the retained-earnings requirement;
5. the approved distribution percentage;
6. the recipient eligibility rules;
7. the distribution asset, method, and date;
8. the approving transaction or governance record.

The sustainable goal is not a permanently high headline APY. It is a growing, well-managed pool of productive protocol-owned capital.
